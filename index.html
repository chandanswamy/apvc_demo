Welcome to Andhra Pradesh Vigilance Commission
Origin & Evolution

The Andhra Pradesh Vigilance Commission came into being on 27 June 1964, as an independent advisory body to deal with prevention of corruption and maintenance of integrity in public services, ensuring just and fair exercise of administrative powers vested in authorities under the State Government.

It’s primary objective is to advise the Government on matters relating to corruption, misconduct, lack of integrity or other malpractices or misdemeanour, on the part of public servants under the executive power of the State.

Abolition & Revival

The Commission was abolished vide G.O.Ms.No.72, G.A. (SC.E) Department, dated 03-02-1983, creating a special authority called “Dharma Mahamatra”.

With the enforcement of the Andhra Pradesh Lok Ayukta and Upa Lok Ayukta Act, 1983, the Commission was formally abolished on 18-08-1984.

Based on the recommendations of the Committee on Administrative Re-organisation, the Commission was revived through G.O.Ms. No.368, G.A. (SC.D) Department, dated 29-06-1993. It re-started functioning from 30 June 1993 with the same jurisdiction and responsibilities.

Jurisdiction

The scheme of the Vigilance Commission approved in G.O.Ms.No. 421, G.A. (SC.D) Department, dated 03-08-1993 defines the jurisdiction and functions of the Commission.

The Commission may:

  • Cause inquiry into any transaction involving improper or corrupt actions by public servants (including AIS officers).
  • Investigate complaints of corruption, misconduct, lack of integrity or criminal misconduct under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
  • Call for records, reports, and statements from all the Government Departments, undertakings, and notified autonomous bodies.
  • Exercise general superintendence over vigilance and anti-corruption work.
  • Direct the Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) to register and investigate cases.
  • Entrust complaints to the concerned Department/Public Undertaking for inquiry.

The Commission enjoys autonomy similar to the Andhra Pradesh Public Service Commission. If the Government proposes to deviate from its advice, the case must be circulated to the Chief Minister through the Chief Secretary and Minister-in-charge with reference to clause (ii) of item (14) of III Schedule of A.P. Govt. Business Rules, 2018 r/w Instruction No.69 of Secretariat Instructions.

The Commission submits an Annual Report to the Government G.A. (SC.F) Department, highlighting important recommendations and specifying reasons for non-acceptance of any advice.

Procedure

Procedural instructions were issued with concurrence of Government, vide Letter No. 66/VC.A2/93-3, dated 10-10-1994. These instructions specify:

  • Authorities competent to receive complaints.
  • Procedure for enquiry by Department or ACB.
  • Requirement of First Stage Advice (FSA) on enquiry reports.
  • Requirement of Second Stage Advice (SSA) on proposed penalties.
  • Roles of CVOs, Vigilance Officers, and Collectors.

Further, guidelines were issued in Memo. No.235/Spl.B/2001-1, dated:26-07-2001, classifying cases as:

  • Vigilance Cases: Bribery, illegal gratification, causing loss to Government, fraud, cheating, misappropriation, unlawful gains, criminal misconduct.
  • Non-Vigilance Cases: administrative lapses without vigilance angle; these need not be referred to the Commission.
