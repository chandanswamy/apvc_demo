Summary of Deviation notes for the month of May 2007

 
1.

        
            Disciplinary action was initiated against Smt.K.Leelavathi, Deputy Director of Adult Education (Retired on 29-02-2000) based on the financial irregularities noticed by the then Director of Adult Education, Hyderabad, while conducting arrear and current inspection of O/o. the Deputy Director of Adult Education, Khammam and test check on the expenditure items of Zilla Saksharatha Samithi, Khammam and general review of T.L.C./ P.L.C./ C.E. from 10-02-2000 to 12-02-2000. 

 Commission in its advice dated 04-11-2006 has observed that the crux of the charges is irregularities in implementation of total literacy campaign during 1991-2000 and non settlement of accounts and the Charged Officer made no efforts to settle the accounts during her tenure.  Permanent assets like Mahindra Zeep, Generator should have not been purchased under total literacy programme.  Irregular acquisition of capital assets to the tune of Rs.5,58,345-00 should have been avoided. Open Tender System in procurement of materials like paper for printing T.L.C. primers at cost of Rs.56,24,696/- was not followed. The department was advised to impose the penalty of 15% cut in pension permanently and stoppage of gratuity on Smt.K. Leelavathi, Dy.Director, Adult Education (Retired), duly following the procedure under Rule 9 of A.P.R.P.R., 1980.  But, the Department has issued orders in G.O.Rt.No.289, dt.20-4-2007 dropping further action against her, which is a deviation against the advice of the V.C. (Department is being requested to refer the connected file).       

 
2.

  
            A case against Sri B.Anjaiah, CI of Police, Narasingi Circle of Cyberabad City was registered for being in possession of assets disproportionate to the known sources of income on 29-5-2003 and enquired by the Anti Corruption Bureau. As per preliminary report dt.27-8-03 there is substantial evidence to show that the A.O. is in possession of disproportionate assets worth Rs.65,59,434/-.  The A.O. was placed under suspension by the DG & IG.

            On the advice of the Commission, Government in G.O.Ms.No.221, Home Dept. dt.6-10-2004 accorded sanction to the I.O. of the Bureau for filing an application before the competent court for attachment of properties of the A.O. and his family members worth Rs.52,02,447/-.  The Bureau filed an application before the competent court on 28-10-04 and the court issued ad-interim attachment order on 1-11-2004.
            After examination of final report of the D.G., A.C.B. dt.15-2-06, Commission in letter dt.22-12-2006 advised the department  to prosecute the A.O. in a court of law duly revising the list of disproportionate assets and also to initiate departmental action as recommended by the Bureau.  Govt. in G.O.Rt.No.650, Home Dept. dt.11-5-2007 instead of prosecuting him in a Court of Law on the disproportionate assets cases decided to take departmental action for not filing Annual Property Returns all through from the year 1976 till date and also for not taking proper permission to acquire immovable and movable properties.
 
3.

         
           Sri Vangala Nageswara Babu, Assistant Executive Engineer, Panchayat Raj, Amrutalur mandal, Guntur district was caught red-handed on 23-8-2006, when he demanded and accepted a bribe amount of Rs.10,000/- from the complainant, Sri Chadalavada Ramesh Babu for processing bills for works.  He himself came to complainant after a phone call and accepted the tainted amount.  At the time of post trap proceedings, the accused officer himself admitted having received the amount and took out a wad of currency notes from his upper left side shirt pocket and produced before the Mediators.  In view of the clinching and irrefutable evidences, such as, positive chemical test, recovery of tainted amount from the shirt pocket of the A.O., corroborating 161 & 164 Cr.P.C. statements of the complainant, immediate reply of the A.O., Commission on 11-1-2007 advised the Panchayat Raj & Rural Development Department to prosecute the A.O. in a court of law for the offences punishable under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.  Further, the A.O. in his explanation in reply to the statutory notice averred that he would disclose his defence only in the Honourable Court at appropriate time.  But, the department on 28-5-2007 issued orders entrusting the case to the Tribunal for Disciplinary Proceedings for inquiry into the allegations.  Thus the department deviated from the advice of the Commission.
