Summary of Deviation notes for the month of July- 2007

 
1.

The Anti-Corruption Bureau found that Sri T.S.R.Anjaneyulu, formerly Addl. Commissioner (Admn), MCH is  involved in a case of possessing disproportionate assets to a tune of Rs.88,06,049.00. Commission advised the department (a) to place him under suspension, (b)to  issue authorization in favour of the Investigating officer of ACB for attachment of the money and other property  and (c)to prosecute him in a court of law besides  initiating  departmental action against him. But the Department has not issued orders to that effect   on the ground  that there are certain discrepancies in computing the income, expenditure and savings of the officer, and also keeping in view of his sincerity, meritorious and outstanding track record of the officer, have decided to drop further action and accordingly dropped action against him vide G.O.Ms.No:495, dt.10.7.2007,  in total deviation of Commission’s advises.

 
2.

Departmental inquiry was conducted against  Sri A. Suryanarayana, E.E.(Retd.)on the irregularities  in  execution of works of CPWS schemes in Vontimitta and Pondaluru in Kadapa Dist.  The charges are that  that he failed to discharge his duties  in conducting  prescribed hydraulic test  resulting  in leakages of pipes and water not flowing through pipes thus the water was never delivered to the habitations / hamlets and also that lead to an additional expenditure of  Rs.60 lakhs  to Government       The Inquiry Authority held that the  charges against  Sri A. Suryanarayana, EE  proved.   On examination of the Inquiry  Authority’s report,  Commission advised the department in letter dated: 27-9-2006 to  recover an amount of Rs.1,65,239/-  from him towards the 1/3rd  share of revised total  loss of Rs.4,95,718/- besides withholding of 50% pension permanently  on him.   Though the  charges held proved against the  retired Executive Engineer is serious in nature, the Panchayati Raj & Rural Development Department issued orders  vide G.O.Rt.No.1026 P.R.& R.D.(Vig.II) Dept., dt. 24-7-2007 withholding   of 50%  pension for 5 years only  instead of permanently  in  deviation from the advice of the Commission. Recovery   of  Rs.1,65,239/-  is ordered to be recovered from his gratuity by the Government in the same order.

 
3.

During a Surprise Check conducted by the Officials of the Anti-Corruption Bureau, Sri K. Subrahmanyam, former Municipal Commissioner, Kakinada was found in possesion  of a huge amount of Rs. 1,28,400/-  while on travel from Kakinada to Hyderabad for which he could not give any valid and satisfactory explanation.  The investigations conducted by the ACB confirmed that the said amount was nothing but illegal gratifacation secured by the A.O. from the contractors.  Considering the overwhelming evidence revealed by the Bureau`s investigations, Commission concurred with the Bureau`s recommendations and advised the department for according prosecution on 17.4.2006 .  But , in its initial response, the department , instead of prosecution, took a decision to initiate departmental action against the accused.  Subsequently, the Minister proposed  on 21.5.2006 for even dropping the proposed departmental action on the ground that the accused was not given reasonable opportunity to explain on the points raised by the Bureau and that he is retiring within a year`s time.  Though on this proposal, both the CS and the CM seconded the VC`s opinion only i.e., for the prosecution of the accused, the issue was re-examined with reference to a representation forwarded by the Minister of Major Irrigation.  Then, it was opined by the Minister for M.A. again that the accused was neither trapped nor found in possession of disproportionate assets and that there are no strong grounds warranting his prosecution. On this,  Chief Minister agreed with the views of the Chief Secretary that it may not be possible to sustain a prosecution in this case and passed orders for the initiation of departmental action only against the accused which is in deviation of VC`s  advice.
