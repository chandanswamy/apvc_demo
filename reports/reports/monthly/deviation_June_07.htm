Summary of Deviation notes for the month of June- 2007

 
1.

        
            Sri E.Raghuramaiah, Labour Officer, O/o the Asst.Commissioner of Labour, Nellore was trapped on 15.9.2003 when he demanded and accepted illegal gratification of Rs.1000/- from the complainant for doing official favour. 

         Based on the final report submitted by the DG, ACB the Department was advised to  place the Labour Officer on his defence before TDP. The Government entrusted the case to TDP. The TDP in its report stated that charge of demand and acceptance against the Labour Officer, is not established by the prosecution. Therefore, the AO is found not guilty of misconduct within the meaning of Rule 2 (b) of The AP Civil Services (DPT) Rules, 1989 and he is entitled for exoneration fully.

         Commission observed from the TDP report that the fact of pendency of petition filed by PW-1 is established.  The CO has not given satisfactory explanation for keeping silence on the petition for a very long time, though he gave two adjournments,  after that he kept silent.  The trap money was found in the table drawer of CO.  Neither the mediator report nor the mediator mentioned the presence of DW-1.  The presence of DW-1, Advocate, at the time of trap has to be doubted and that no prudent person will keep the tainted money in CO’s table drawer especially when a third party that too before an Advocate being physically present in the room.  The evidence of the clerk bringing the file after approach by PW-1 and C.O. giving adjournment date on it lends support of prosecution allegation of C.O. doing favour only after receipt of illegal gratification.  In view of the above, Commission advised the department to disagree with the findings of TDP and to communicate the disagreement factors to the Charged Officer for submission of his written statement and to examine the same and arrive at the punishment to be imposed

         However, the department have deviated from the advice of this Commission by obtaining orders in circulation on the ground that the matter was elaborately dealt by TDP and finally held that the Charged Officer not guilty on any count and recommended for his exoneration and passed orders to drop further action against Sri E.Raghuramaiah, Labour Officer, vide G.O.Ms.No.47, LET & F Dept., dt. 26.5.2007, instead of communicating disagreement factors to the Charged Officer for submission of his written representation.

         Government without taking the totality of facts into consideration, ordered to drop further action against him, which is in deviation of the advice of Vigilance Commission in this case.  Hence included in the Annual Report for 2006-07. 

 
2.

  Sri G.Chandrasekhar, M.C. LB Nagar Municipality was trapped on 15.12.2004, when he demanded and accepted a bribe of Rs.500/- from the complainant Sri B.Pramod Kumar for doing official favour for according permission and approving the plan for the new house of the latter.  The A.O. was placed under suspension by the CDMA vide his proceedings dt.16.12.2004.

Meanwhile, the Bureau has also registered a case against Sri G.Chandrasekhar Mpl. Commissioner, LB Nagar Mplty., for being  in possession of assets disproportionate to the known sources of his income, vide Cr.No:4/ACB-HR/2004 dt.3.3.2005. 

            Subsequently,  based on the final report of A.C.B., in trap case  Commission advised the Department on 5.5.2005 to prosecute  A.O Sri G.Chandrasekhar, Municipal Commissioner, L.B. Nagar Municipality, R.R. District, in a Court of law under section 7, and 13(2) read with 13(1(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.

However, the Department have decided to decline the request of D.G. A.C.B. to accord sanction for prosecuting the A.O. in Trap case, and issued orders to initiate departmental action only against him in the Trap case, instead  of prosecution.   Though there were no orders passed by M [MA] /C.M. Orders were issued vide G.O.Rt.No:1489 M.A. & U.D. Deptt. dt.31.10.2005 that the request of D.G., A.C.B. for attachment of properties in the disproportionate assets case against the A.O. is not agreed to.  In this connection, Commission advised the Department on 3.12.2005 that the concerned S.O. and A.S.O. of M.A. & U.D. Deptt. who have drafted the G.O. Rt.No:1489, M.A. & U.D. Deptt. dt.31.10.2005 with ulterior motive to benefit the accused officer, may be shifted at once from the Vigilance Section and posted in non-focal post.   Further, Commission advised the Department to direct the concerned C.V.O./A.S. of the Department to scrutinize the drafts carefully and to ensure that the orders issued are in tune with the orders in circulation.

 Further, in the assets case, Commission advised the Department on 12.5.2005 to issue authorisation to the Investigating officer of Bureau to secure attachment of money and property of the accused officer under section 3 and 4 of the Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance 1944, and for making an application before a competent court for attachment of the money and other property to a tune of Rs.49,72,724/- immediately.  Subsequently, based on the final report received from the Bureau in the assets case, Commission advised the Department on  7.6.2006  to prosecute him in a court of law under section 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e) of P.C.Act, 1988 also to initiate departmental action against him for his failure to furnish the Annual Property Returns from the date of entry into service till 2003.  In respect of departmental action for violation of conduct rules, the Department has issued orders duly framing charges against him vide G.O.Rt.No:958, dt.28.8.2006. Govt. have also issued orders authorising the Investigating Officer of ACB for attachment of properties of the A.O. vide G.O.Ms.No:206, dt.29.3.2007. Further, the Bureau has also filed charge sheet on 7.6.2007  against him in the Court of the Addl. Spl. Judge for SPE & ACB cases, Hyderabad, in the assets case.

Subsequently, in the Trap case, the Department have issued orders  vide G.O.Rt.No:805, MA&U D Deptt. dt.25.6.2007  dropping all  further action i.e. departmental proceedings against Sri  G.Chandrasekhar, Mpl. Commissioner LB Nagar Municipality without taking V.C. into confidence, which itself is a very serious deviation of the advice of the Commission dt.5.5.2005.

In the disproportionate assets case also,  the Department have issued orders withdrawing the prosecution orders and also orders issued for attachment of properties of A.O. vide G.O.Ms.No:459, dt.25.6.2007 & 460, dt.25.6.2007 also without taking V.C. into confidence and in violation of relevant rules. Thus Government ignored the V.C’s advices in both these cases which is very unfortunate.           

 
3.

A case u/s 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e)of the PC Act, 1988 was registered on 11-12-2001 against Sri Bevara Durga Prsad, AEE, G.Sigadam mandal, Srikakulam district.  On receipt of the preliminary report, the accused officer was transferred to Araku of Visakhapatnam.

As per the Final Report, the AO was in possession of disproportionate assets to a tune of Rs.6,99,082/-.  On filing the charge sheet by the ACB on 23-8-2005 in the Court of Special Judge for ACB Cases, Visakhapatnam, the ENC, PR on 26-4-2006 issued orders placing the AO under suspension in terms of clause © of sub-rule (1) of rule 8of APCS (CC&A) Rules, 1991.  The APAT on 8-10-2006 while allowing the O.A.No.3704 of2006 filed by the AO, set aside the impugned orders of suspension.

Commission on 5-3-2007 while rebutting the views of the APAT, giving strong grounds and citing decisions of the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India and High Courts,  which are in favour of the Government, advised the department to file an appeal in the High Court of Andhra Pradesh, as the Tribunal’s orders have far-reaching repercussions, if not questioned.  But, the department instead of filing appeal in the Hon’ble High Court of Andhra Pradesh, issued orders reinstating the accused officer into service.

Thus the department deviated from the advice of the Commission.
